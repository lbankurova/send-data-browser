SENDEX

From SEND data to a defensible safety conclusion -- faster

Preclinical toxicologists spend days manually screening endpoints, building dose-response charts, cross-referencing clinical path with histopath, and assembling the weight-of-evidence narrative that ultimately supports a NOAEL. Most of that work is mechanical -- and most of it can be automated without taking the scientist out of the loop.

SENDEX does the heavy lifting. Load your SEND data and get automated signal detection, dose-response analysis, syndrome identification, NOAEL determination, and recovery assessment -- all with transparent reasoning you can inspect, challenge, and override. The expert judgement is yours. The computation is ours.

15 SEND domains analyzed. 47 cross-domain syndromes. 30 compound class profiles. 9 confidence dimensions per finding. 6 species with interpretation rules. 5 study type adapters.

Study design

Before you can trust any analysis, you need to know the tool understood your study correctly. SENDEX parses control groups, dose groups, TK satellites, recovery arms, and multi-compound layouts from your SEND data and shows you every interpretation decision it made -- with confidence levels. When the sponsor mislabeled a control or the CRO used a non-standard arm coding, you see the flag before it corrupts every downstream comparison.

  1. Study loads -> engine makes 12 pre-analysis decisions automatically
  2. StudyBanner shows species, design type, dose groups, TK count, multi-compound badge
  3. Provenance messages (Prov-001 to Prov-011) flag decisions with info/warning/error icons
  4. MortalityBanner shows deaths, early-death toggle, mortality LOAEL
  5. Scientist reviews decisions, corrects via overrides (when available)

Signal detection

Manually screening hundreds of endpoints across 15 domains for treatment-related signals is the most time-consuming part of study evaluation. SENDEX scores every finding for treatment-relatedness using a 9-dimension confidence model -- statistical significance, dose-response pattern, cross-sex concordance, historical control context, and more -- so the findings most likely to matter surface first. You still make the call; you just don't have to hunt.

  1. FindingsRail groups by organ/syndrome/finding/specimen; signal score ranks by evidence
  2. Center panel: DoseResponseChartPanel (continuous) or IncidenceDoseCharts (MI/MA/CL)
  3. Context panel: VerdictPane narrative, GradeConfidencePane (D1-D9), EvidencePane (stats)
  4. DayStepper navigates timepoints; day labels auto-generated (D15 peak, D29 terminal)

Dose-response

Deciding whether a finding is treatment-related often comes down to the dose-response pattern: is there a clear monotonic increase with dose? Does the effect appear at the same timepoint across groups? SENDEX gives you interactive dose-response charts for every endpoint -- continuous and incidence -- with per-animal traces, time-course trajectories, and distribution plots so you can see the full picture without building separate analyses.

  1. D-R chart: bar/line with CI for continuous, horizontal bars for incidence
  2. Time course pane: dose-group trajectories (4 Y-axis modes: g, absolute, %baseline, %control)
  3. Subject trace overlay: per-animal polylines on group trajectory, click opens profile
  4. Day stepper: navigate across timepoints (D15, D29 terminal, D50 recovery)
  5. Distribution pane: strip plot with per-subject dots, group mean/SD whisker

Cross-domain patterns

A liver signal is stronger when ALT, AST, liver weight, and hepatocellular hypertrophy all point the same direction. SENDEX detects 47 cross-domain syndromes by correlating clinical pathology, histopathology, organ weights, and body weight -- with species-specific interpretation rules for dog, NHP, rabbit, and guinea pig physiology. The patterns you would assemble mentally across spreadsheets are computed and presented as a coherent picture.

  1. Rail syndrome grouping: select a syndrome, view all participating endpoints across domains
  2. SyndromeContextPanel: co-variation summary, pharmacological context card
  3. CorrelationsPane: organ correlation matrix
  4. EndpointSyndromePane: per-endpoint syndrome membership (SI+, SI-, U)
  5. Organ analytics: target organ coherence, proportionality scoring

Weight of evidence

Arriving at a defensible NOAEL is the core deliverable of most preclinical studies, yet the weight-of-evidence reasoning behind it often lives only in the toxicologist's head. SENDEX makes that reasoning explicit: which findings drive the NOAEL, how robust is it to individual animal exclusion, what is the confidence across statistical, dose-response, and historical control dimensions. When your expert judgement differs, you override the computed NOAEL and the rationale is preserved alongside the data.

  1. NoaelDeterminationPane: combined/M/F NOAEL, LOAEL, confidence breakdown, PK exposure
  2. SafetyMarginCalculator: TRD / NOAEL = safety margin
  3. VerdictPane: WoE synthesis (core triad + supporting + floor, D-R confidence)
  4. Override dropdown: manually set NOAEL to different dose (persisted annotation)
  5. StudyStatementsBar: 'NOAEL established at [dose]' banner

Recovery

"Did the finding reverse?" is a question asked for every adverse effect, and answering it usually means comparing terminal and recovery group means in a spreadsheet. SENDEX computes recovery verdicts automatically -- effect-size comparisons between cohorts, species-adjusted expected durations, and per-subject tracking -- so you can assess reversibility for every finding without manual calculation.

  1. RecoveryDumbbellChart: before/after comparison per endpoint
  2. IncidenceRecoveryChart: time-series recovery verdicts (compact mode for panes)
  3. RecoveryPane: verdict + duration context per finding
  4. Recovery override via right-click dropdown on findings table

Subject-level analysis

When a NOAEL hinges on one or two animals, you need to know who they are and whether they're outliers or sentinels. SENDEX provides leave-one-out sensitivity analysis (exclude an animal, see how conclusions change), outlier and sentinel detection (Qn z-scores, Hamada residuals, Everds triad), similarity clustering, and per-animal profiles that show every measurement and observation for a subject in one place. The question "which animals are driving this result" gets a concrete, quantitative answer.

  1. CohortView: similarity scatter (Gower MDS) + subject signal table
  2. Influence mode: heatmap of animals x endpoints, per-animal importance
  3. Sentinel overlays: COC ring, sole-finding triangle, disposition cross
  4. Click subject -> SubjectProfilePanel: measurements, observations, findings, radar
  5. LOO sensitivity pane: per-animal stability, exclude/recompute workflow

Cross-study comparison

Program-level safety assessment requires comparing findings across studies -- often conducted years apart at different CROs with different pathologists using different terminology. SENDEX normalizes pathologist language through a multi-source term recognition pipeline so the same finding is recognized across studies. Cross-study views for concordance, safety margins, and program-level dashboards are in active development.

  1. Landing page: study list with NOAEL badges, validation status, context menu
  2. Portfolio view: study table with filters, program context panel
  3. NO cross-study analytical views currently exposed in UI

Data quality

Bad data produces bad conclusions, and SEND datasets frequently arrive with coding errors, missing domains, or terminology inconsistencies. SENDEX runs study design checks, FDA-style data quality rules, CDISC CORE compliance validation, and domain-specific integrity checks before analysis begins -- so you catch problems before they become wrong conclusions.

  1. ValidationView: rule-level summary rail, per-record drill-down
  2. Fix guidance per rule, severity classification (Error/Warning/Info)
  3. Affected records table: fix status, review status, annotation
  4. CDISC CORE engine: version-aware (SENDIG 3.0 / 3.1)
