## Analytical Answers

### 1. What is the denominator used for the Total (%) column?

**The denominator is 570 (total animals in the study).**

Verification: The ABSTRACT states "Data were collected from 570 monkeys (285 animals per sex)". The reported percentages in Total (%) columns match this calculation:
- TABLE 2, HEART, inflammatory cell foci: 147 animals / 570 animals = 25.8% ✓
- TABLE 3, LIVER, inflammatory cell foci: 346 animals / 570 animals = 60.7% ✓
- TABLE 4, STOMACH, gastritis: 70 animals / 570 animals = 12.3% ✓
- TABLE 5, KIDNEY, inflammatory cell infiltrations: 164 animals / 570 animals = 28.8% ✓

### 2. What does the (n) annotation after organ names mean?

**The (n) value indicates the number of studies (out of 60 total studies) in which that organ had a recorded finding.**

From the footnote a on each table: "Number of studies with a recorded finding in the organ"

For example:
- "LUNG (55)" means inflammatory findings in the lung were recorded in 55 of the 60 studies
- "HEART (50)" means heart findings were recorded in 50 of the 60 studies
- "TESTIS (5)" means testis findings were recorded in only 5 of the 60 studies

This reflects that not all organs were examined in all studies (some may have been excluded based on study design), and not all findings appear in every study.

### 3. For male-only anatomical findings in a both-sex organ, what denominator does the paper use?

**For sex-specific organs in both-sex animals (e.g., testis, prostate, epididymis, ovary), the denominator is 285 (the sex-appropriate animals only).**

Verification:
- TABLE 5, TESTIS, hypoplasia of seminiferous tubules: Male: 4, Female: –, Total: 4, Pct: 1.4%
  - 4 animals / 285 males = 1.40% ✓
- TABLE 5, PROSTATE, inflammatory cell infiltrates: Male: 9, Female: –, Total: 9, Pct: 3.2%
  - 9 animals / 285 males = 3.16% ≈ 3.2% ✓
- TABLE 5, OVARY, cysts: Male: –, Female: 25, Total: 25, Pct: 8.8%
  - 25 animals / 285 females = 8.77% ≈ 8.8% ✓

This makes logical sense: sex-specific organs cannot appear in animals of the opposite sex, so the denominator is limited to the relevant sex group of 285 animals.

## Additional Observations

1. The paper uses ranges that are described as "determined only in studies with five to eight animals per group" (footnote b). This subset restriction likely reflects the focus on smaller studies.

2. Some findings have a dash (–) for one sex, indicating either:
   - Zero occurrences in that sex (when "0" is used, like LIVER ectopic adrenal gland: 0 males, 2 females)
   - The organ is sex-specific and thus not applicable (like TESTIS findings where females are marked –)

3. Some findings with very low counts show "Range (%)" as a dash (–) rather than a percentage range, likely because there were insufficient studies with 5-8 animals per group to calculate reliable ranges (TABLE 4, STOMACH, gastric infarction/necrosis shows 3 total animals but no range).

## Total Findings Extracted

118 individual findings across 7 tables (TABLE 2-7), representing pathology observations from 570 cynomolgus macaques (285 males, 285 females) across 60 regulatory toxicology studies conducted 2003-2009.
