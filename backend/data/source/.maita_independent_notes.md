## Unusual Features of the Paper

1. **Bilingual structure:** The paper is published in English (pages 0-4) with a complete Japanese-language summary/title section (pages 5-6). The Japanese section is a translation/summary and does NOT introduce new data.

2. **High prevalence of gallstones:** 87.5% is remarkably high and noted as a common spontaneous change in beagles used in toxicity studies.

3. **Temporal finding:** The paper notes that "Intranuclear hyaline inclusions and lipofuscin deposition were more frequently seen in dogs after being subjected to tests for more than 13 weeks," suggesting testing duration affects lesion frequency. However, no specific percentages are given for this temporal comparison (this is descriptive only).

4. **Parasitic infection endemic:** Despite parasite control measures (parbendazole given at 2, 4 weeks of age and monthly thereafter), 33.1% still showed Toxocara canis larvae migration lesions, indicating incomplete eradication.

5. **Prostate inflammation:** The paper notes prostatitis "pathogenesis remained unclear" — suggesting this is an incidental finding without clear etiology.

6. **Behavioral/stress-related necrosis:** The paper notes hepatic necrosis "seemed to occur more frequently in either animals exciting and not obedient in handling or those being subjected frequently to long time-holding," suggesting stress as a contributor.
